Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace is often called the world’s first computer programmer. Back in the 1800s — way before electronic computers — she wrote an algorithm for a machine that didn’t even fully exist yet. She had this amazing ability to see how numbers could be used for more than just math — like creating music or art. Her vision was way ahead of her time.

Alan Turing

Alan Turing is one of the most important figures in computer science. During World War II, he helped crack secret enemy codes using early computer-like machines. That work saved millions of lives and also laid the groundwork for what we now call artificial intelligence. Without Turing, modern computing might not even exist.

Grace Hopper

Grace Hopper was a computer scientist and Navy Rear Admiral who helped invent one of the first programming languages. She believed that computers should be able to understand human-like commands, not just ones and zeros. Thanks to her work, programming became much easier and more accessible to everyone.

More Modern Names

There are tons of other people who’ve made big impacts — like Bill Gates (Microsoft), Steve Jobs (Apple), and Tim Berners-Lee (the web). Each of them helped shape how we use technology today — from the software we run to the devices we carry in our pockets.
